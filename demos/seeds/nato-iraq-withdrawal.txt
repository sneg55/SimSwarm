NATO WITHDRAWAL FROM IRAQ — March 2026

US Air Force General Alexus Grynkewich announced that NATO has withdrawn troops from Iraq and will relocate from the Middle East to Europe amid the 2026 Iran war. Polish Defence Minister Wladyslaw Kosiniak-Kamysz confirmed the withdrawal of Polish forces from Iraq, previously stationed as part of the CJTF-OIR joint task force against the Islamic State.

Concurrent militia activity in Iraq:
- The Islamic Resistance in Iraq claimed responsibility for targeting 27 American military bases in Iraq and across the region over 24 hours on March 21.
- One Iraqi police officer was killed in a drone strike on the headquarters of the Iraqi National Intelligence Service in Baghdad.
- Fifteen fighters from Iraq's Popular Mobilization Forces (PMF), including a commander, were killed in an American airstrike on a PMF base in Al Anbar Governorate on March 24. A follow-up strike hit the same base the next day, killing 7 more and injuring 13.
- Kata'ib Hezbollah paused strikes on the US embassy in Baghdad, extending the pause by five days as negotiations progressed.

Regional context:
- The US has struck over 8,000 targets in Iran since the war began.
- Iran has launched ballistic missile attacks on Israel, Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait, and Jordan.
- Kuwait, Saudi Arabia, and UAE report intercepting Iranian missile and drone attacks.
- Lebanon expelled its Iranian ambassador over Iran's support of Hezbollah. The Lebanon war death toll from Israeli attacks since March 2 has risen to 1,039.
- Uganda's military chief said the country is ready to join the war "on the side of Israel."

The withdrawal raises questions about the power vacuum in Iraq, ISIS resurgence risks, and the role of Iran-aligned militias in filling the security gap.

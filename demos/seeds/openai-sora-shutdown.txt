OPENAI SHUTS DOWN SORA — March 24, 2026

American technology company OpenAI announced it will shut down its Sora video generation app. The Walt Disney Company's $1 billion investment deal to allow Disney's intellectual properties to be used with Sora was also cancelled.

Timeline of Sora:
- February 2024: OpenAI previewed Sora, a text-to-video AI model capable of generating realistic 60-second videos from text prompts.
- December 2024: Sora launched publicly with a subscription model.
- 2025: Disney signed a $1 billion deal to integrate Disney IP (Marvel, Star Wars, Pixar characters) with Sora for consumer video creation.
- March 24, 2026: OpenAI announces shutdown. Disney deal cancelled.

Industry context:
- Competitors Runway, Pika, and Google's Veo continue to operate in the AI video space.
- The AI video generation market was projected to reach $1.3 billion by 2027.
- Sora faced ongoing copyright lawsuits from content creators and studios whose work was used for training.
- The shutdown comes amid broader AI industry turbulence — Epic Games also announced 1,000+ layoffs the same day.
- OpenAI had been restructuring around its core ChatGPT and API businesses.

Creator ecosystem impact:
- Thousands of creators built workflows and businesses around Sora's capabilities.
- Agencies and studios that adopted Sora for pre-visualization and concept work must migrate to alternatives.
- The Disney cancellation signals corporate risk aversion toward AI-generated content deals.

Key questions: How will the creator ecosystem migrate? Will competitors capture Sora's market share or will the category contract? Does the Disney cancellation signal a broader pullback from AI content licensing deals?

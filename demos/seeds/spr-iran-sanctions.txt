US STRATEGIC PETROLEUM RESERVE RELEASE & IRAN SANCTIONS RELIEF — March 2026

On March 20, 2026, US Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent announced that the US would temporarily lift sanctions on Iranian oil at sea for 30 days (until April 19) to ease energy supply pressures caused by the Strait of Hormuz crisis.

The same week, the US Department of Energy awarded contracts to loan 45.2 million barrels of crude oil from the Strategic Petroleum Reserve (SPR).

Market context:
- Brent crude hit $110/barrel after Iran closed the Strait of Hormuz.
- Oil briefly fell 11% below $100 on March 23 when Trump announced "productive conversations" with Iran.
- Prices rebounded sharply when Iran rejected the US 15-point peace plan.
- Gold prices in Dubai fluctuated amid diplomatic uncertainty.

Prior SPR releases:
- The US released 180 million barrels in 2022 following Russia's invasion of Ukraine, the largest release in SPR history.
- The SPR was at historically low levels going into 2026, with approximately 370 million barrels remaining.
- The current 45.2 million barrel loan represents roughly 12% of remaining reserves.

Iran sanctions relief implications:
- India purchased LPG from Iran for the first time since 2019 following the temporary easing.
- The 30-day window creates uncertainty about what happens when sanctions snap back on April 19.
- Iranian oil production capacity is estimated at 3.2 million barrels per day, though actual exports were restricted to under 1.5 million bpd prior to sanctions relief.
- China and India are the largest buyers of Iranian oil.

Key question: Will the combined SPR release + temporary sanctions relief be enough to stabilize markets, or will the April 19 sanctions snapback and continued Hormuz uncertainty trigger another price spike?

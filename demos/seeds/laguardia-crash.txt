AIR CANADA EXPRESS COLLISION AT LAGUARDIA AIRPORT — March 22, 2026

A Bombardier CRJ900LR operating as Air Canada Express Flight 8646 from Montreal, Quebec, collided with a firetruck while landing at LaGuardia Airport in New York City. Both pilots were killed and 41 others were injured.

Incident details:
- The aircraft was on approach to LaGuardia when it collided with a fire/rescue vehicle on or near the runway.
- The flight originated from Montreal-Trudeau International Airport.
- The CRJ900LR is a regional jet with a typical capacity of 76 passengers.
- Emergency response was complicated by the fact that a fire/rescue vehicle was itself involved in the collision.

Aviation safety context:
- LaGuardia Airport has historically been considered one of the most challenging airports for pilots due to its short runways, water approaches, and dense air traffic.
- A runway incursion between a JetBlue aircraft and a Learjet occurred at LaGuardia in January 2023.
- The FAA has been under scrutiny since the January 2023 NOTAM system failure that grounded all US flights.
- In January 2025, a PSA Airlines/American Eagle flight collided midair with a Black Hawk helicopter while landing at Reagan National Airport, killing 67 people.

Regulatory questions:
- What was a firetruck doing on or near an active runway during an aircraft landing?
- What communication failures occurred between ATC and ground operations?
- Will this incident accelerate calls for modernizing ground vehicle tracking systems at US airports?
- How will this affect Air Canada's operations and the broader regional airline industry?

The NTSB has launched an investigation. The incident is the deadliest US aviation accident since the Reagan National collision in 2025.

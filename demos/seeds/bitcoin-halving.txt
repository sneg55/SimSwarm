BITCOIN BLOCK REWARD HALVING — 2026

Bitcoin block 1,050,000 has been mined, reducing the block reward from 3.125 BTC to 1.5625 BTC. This is the fifth halving in Bitcoin's history, occurring approximately every 210,000 blocks (roughly 4 years).

Price context at halving:
- Bitcoin price at halving: $78,400
- Price 1 month before: $72,100 (8.7% pre-halving rally)
- Previous cycle: price was $64,000 at the April 2024 halving, reached $108,000 by December 2024
- Current market cap: approximately $1.55 trillion
- Daily mining revenue drops from ~$45M to ~$22.5M

Halving history and subsequent price action:
- 2012 halving (50→25 BTC): price went from $12 to $1,100 over 12 months
- 2016 halving (25→12.5 BTC): price went from $650 to $19,700 over 18 months
- 2020 halving (12.5→6.25 BTC): price went from $8,700 to $69,000 over 18 months
- 2024 halving (6.25→3.125 BTC): price went from $64,000 to $108,000 over 8 months, then corrected to $78,000

Mining industry impact:
- Smaller mining operations face profitability pressure at current difficulty levels
- Marathon Digital and Riot Platforms announced efficiency upgrades ahead of halving
- Hash rate expected to temporarily decline 10-15% as unprofitable miners shut down
- Energy cost per BTC mined approximately doubles overnight
- Transaction fees become increasingly important revenue source for miners

Market sentiment:
- Crypto Twitter divided between "supercycle" bulls predicting $200K+ and bears citing diminishing returns each cycle
- Institutional investors (BlackRock, Fidelity ETFs) hold ~5% of circulating supply
- On-chain metrics show long-term holders accumulating, exchange reserves at 5-year lows
- Telegram and Reddit communities debating whether "this time is different" or if halvings are "priced in"
- Derivatives markets show elevated call option activity at $100K and $150K strikes for Q4 2026

GLOBAL ENERGY CRISIS AT $110 OIL — March 2026

Brent crude oil surpassed $110 per barrel on March 27, 2026, after Iran's IRGC Navy closed the Strait of Hormuz. The price briefly fell below $100 on March 23 when Trump announced "productive conversations" with Iran, then rebounded sharply when negotiations stalled.

Country-by-country impact:

EUROPE:
- Norway's parliament passed emergency legislation cutting petrol and diesel taxes in response to surging energy costs.
- Slovenia became the first EU country to introduce fuel rationing to cope with extra demand from drivers crossing from neighboring countries.
- The EU Parliament approved legislation implementing the EU-US trade deal from 2025, partly motivated by energy security coordination.

MIDDLE EAST & AFRICA:
- South Sudan's main electricity distributor in Juba announced daily rotational power cuts starting March 26.
- Mauritius announced electricity consumption restrictions.
- QatarEnergy declared force majeure on some long-term LNG supply contracts worldwide.

ASIA-PACIFIC:
- Philippine President Marcos declared a state of national energy emergency and signed emergency legislation allowing suspension of petroleum excise taxes when prices hit thresholds.
- Philippine Airlines halted all Middle East flights until April 30.
- India purchased LPG from Iran for the first time since 2019 after temporary US sanctions relief.
- COSCO Shipping resumed Far East-to-Gulf container bookings after Iran allowed select vessels through.

UNITED STATES:
- The US Department of Energy awarded contracts to loan 45.2 million barrels from the Strategic Petroleum Reserve.
- Treasury Secretary Bessent temporarily lifted sanctions on Iranian oil at sea for 30 days to ease supply pressure.

Approximately 21% of global daily oil consumption and 25% of global LNG trade passes through the Strait of Hormuz.

IRAN CEASEFIRE NEGOTIATIONS — March 2026

The United States has sent Iran a 15-point peace plan through the Pakistani government, confirmed by US special envoy Steve Witkoff on March 26, 2026.

Key diplomatic developments:
- Pakistani PM Shehbaz Sharif confirmed that Pakistan is mediating indirect Iran-US negotiations and offered to host talks.
- Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi stated Iran seeks to end the war "on its own terms" and will not enter direct negotiations with the US.
- According to Press TV, Iran rejected the initial US proposal and set five conditions for a ceasefire. A senior Iranian official later said Iran is "still reviewing" the proposal.
- Egyptian FM Badr Abdelatty offered Egypt as host for negotiations.
- Trump claimed "major points of agreement" on Iran's nuclear program, though Iran denied any dialogue took place.
- Kata'ib Hezbollah extended its pause on strikes against the US embassy in Baghdad for five more days.
- Trump initially postponed his 48-hour ultimatum by five days, citing "productive conversations," then extended the Hormuz reopening deadline by 10 days to April 6.

Military context:
- US CENTCOM has struck over 8,000 targets in Iran since the war began, including 130 Iranian Navy vessels.
- Israel assassinated IRGC Navy commander Alireza Tangsiri. Iran confirmed IRGC spokesperson Ali Mohammad Naini killed in an Israeli airstrike in Tehran.
- Israeli and US jets struck the Natanz Nuclear Facility.
- A second Marine Expeditionary Unit of 2,200 US Marines is being deployed to the region.
- Iran fired two intermediate-range ballistic missiles at Diego Garcia; one failed in flight, the other was intercepted by a US warship.
- Iran claims intelligence that the US and an unnamed country are planning to invade Kharg Island.

Anti-war protests have occurred in Berlin, Amsterdam, Prague, India, and Newfoundland.

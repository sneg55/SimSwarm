EPIC GAMES LAYS OFF 1,000+ AMID FORTNITE DECLINE — March 24, 2026

American video game company Epic Games announced that it will lay off more than 1,000 employees along with more than $500 million in savings from cuts to contracting and marketing amid a sharp decline in the number of people playing its flagship video game Fortnite.

Key details:
- The layoffs represent approximately 25% of Epic's workforce (estimated at ~4,000 employees before cuts).
- $500 million in additional savings will come from reduced contracting and marketing spend.
- The primary driver is declining Fortnite player counts, though Epic did not disclose specific numbers.
- Fortnite's revenue peaked at $9.1 billion in 2018-2019 and has been declining since.

Industry context — 2022-2026 video game layoffs:
- The gaming industry has shed over 30,000 jobs since 2022.
- Major layoffs include Microsoft/Activision Blizzard (1,900 in 2024), Sony (900 in 2024), EA (670 in 2024), and Riot Games (530 in 2024).
- Epic previously laid off 830 employees in September 2023.
- The Unreal Engine business and Epic Games Store remain significant but have not offset Fortnite's revenue decline.

Fortnite competitive landscape:
- Fortnite faces competition from Roblox, Minecraft, Valorant, and emerging titles.
- The battle royale genre has matured, with player bases fragmenting across multiple games.
- Epic's metaverse ambitions (Fortnite as a social platform) have not generated sufficient new revenue.

Broader implications:
- The Epic Games Store's ability to compete with Steam may be impacted by reduced investment.
- Unreal Engine 5 licensing and support could be affected by workforce reductions.
- Creator ecosystem (Fortnite Creative, UEFN) participants face uncertainty about platform investment.
- The layoffs coincide with OpenAI shutting down Sora the same day, contributing to a "tech winter" narrative.
